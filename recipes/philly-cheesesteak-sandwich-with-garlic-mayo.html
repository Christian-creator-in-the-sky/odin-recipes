Philly Cheesesteak Sandwich with Garlic Mayo

How to Make a Philly Cheesesteak Sandwich

Description

For a delicious Philly Cheesesteak Sandwich with Garlic Mayo, start by preparing the garlic mayo. In a small bowl, mix together mayonnaise, minced garlic, lemon juice, and Dijon mustard (optional), then season with salt and pepper. Set it aside in the fridge while you cook the steak. Thinly slice the ribeye (or sirloin) against the grain, and sauté it in a hot skillet with a bit of olive oil or butter until browned and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes. Remove the steak and set it aside. In the same pan, cook the sliced onions and optional bell peppers until caramelized and softened, then return the steak to the pan and combine everything together.

To assemble the sandwiches, spread a generous amount of garlic mayo on both sides of each hoagie roll. Pile the steak mixture into the rolls, then top with provolone cheese (or American cheese for a more classic Philly version). Toast the sandwiches under the broiler for a few minutes until the cheese melts and the rolls are golden. Serve hot, sliced in half, and enjoy your perfectly balanced Philly Cheesesteak with creamy, flavorful garlic mayo!

Ingredients for Philly Cheesesteak Sandwich with Garlic Mayo

For the Sandwich

  • 1 lb ribeye steak (or sirloin), thinly sliced against the grain
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or butter (for cooking)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (optional), thinly sliced
  • 8 oz (about 2 cups) provolone cheese
  • 4 hoagie rolls or soft sub rolls
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Garlic Mayo

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps to Make Philly Cheesesteak Sandwich with Garlic May

  1. Make the Garlic Mayo
    2.
    1. In a small bowl, combine 1/2 cup mayonnaise, 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tbsp lemon juice, and 1 tsp Dijon mustard (if using).
    2. Stir until smooth and season with salt and pepper to taste.
    3. Set aside in the fridge while you prepare the rest of the sandwich.

  2. Prepare the Steak
    3.
    1. Heat 1-2 tbsp olive oil or butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    2. Add 1 large thinly sliced onion (and bell pepper, if using) to the skillet and sauté for 5-7 minutes until softened and lightly caramelized. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
    3. In the same skillet, add the thinly sliced 1 lb ribeye steak. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the steak is browned and cooked through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    4. Return the cooked onions and peppers to the skillet with the steak and mix together.

  3. Assemble the Sandwich
    4.
    1. Preheat the broiler or heat a separate pan for toasting the rolls.
    2. Slice 4 hoagie rolls lengthwise, leaving them hinged on one side.
    3. Spread a generous amount of the garlic mayo on both sides of the rolls.
    4. Pile the cooked steak, onions, and peppers into each roll, ensuring it’s evenly distributed.
    5. Top each sandwich with 2 oz (about 1/2 cup) of provolone cheese.

  4. Toast and Serve
    5.
    1. Place the assembled sandwiches under the broiler for 2-3 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the rolls are lightly toasted.
    2. Remove from the oven, slice the sandwiches in half, and serve immediately. Enjoy your delicious Philly Cheesesteak with Garlic Mayo!
